Objective

The world of Weyard is dying. To save it, you must ascend the four elemental lighthouses and defeat the boss monsters at their aeries!

This game is meant to be played as a campaign. It can be played all in one sitting, or one chapter at a time. There are places to stop and save progress after each of the four chapters, each being one of the four lighthouses. They increase in difficulty as you go on, and carry over equipment and magic from previous chapters.

The game can be played with 1-4 players, but is designed around a full 4 player party.

Setting up

  1. Lay out the Psynergy, Summon, Puzzle, and Chapter 1 Djinn deck next to the board. Take out any duplicates from the Psynergy deck so that it only has one of each spell, set the extras off to the side.
  2. Decide which chapter you are playing. Put the party token on the tracker on the bottom floor of that chapter’s lighthouse. This may not be the floor that says start depending on the chapter.
  3. Lay out the floor deck and monster deck for the chapter you are playing. Separate any Boss monsters and floors with red key icons out of these decks and lay them off to the side, then shuffle the floor and monster decks. Add all Djinn decks up to that chapter to the chapter 1 deck on the board. If playing chapter 1, leave it at just the basic chapter 1 deck.
  4. Build the item deck by taking green item cards and following the deck recipe for that chapter. It will specify how many of each card to include. Also add the psynergy items that are listed on the card
  5. Add the equipment for the chapter you’re playing to the item deck, this will be labelled in the bottom right corner, each chapter additionally has a unique background.
  6. Shuffle the item deck and place it next to the board. It should have 100 cards exactly.
  7. Each player selects a character. Each player gets a starting equipment, which comes from the separate “chapter 0” starting item deck, and one consumable or psynergy item. If it is not a psynergy item, take this from the leftover cards not put into the chapter’s item deck. Each player also takes a copy of their starting psynergy (if they have any) and searches the djinni deck for their starting djinni (if they have one)
  8. Each player takes 1 die of their characters element, and the dice shown on their weapon card to build their starting dice pool.

Characters

To start, each player selects a character. This is your “adept”. Their Adept Profile is a card that shows their starting stats and equipment. Most characters start the game with a specific psynergy or djinni, take those from their respective decks and lay them near your character card.

On the front face of a character card, their maximum HP and PP is listed for each chapter, as well, most characters have a stat that changes per chapter. For most character this is the ATK stat! Pay attention to this as you move past the first chapter.

On the back face of the character card is the character’s unique ability. Every character has one, and the more mechanically complex characters’ rules are outlined on the back. “Condition”s are negative/neutral consequences that are always in effect surrounding your character. Permanent passives (described later) are positive/neutral effects that give you something as part of a regular mechanic.

Constructing your dice pool

Most of Lighthouse Dash! Is played by rolling various types of dice. These are gathered in a “pool” of dice that are available to you specifically. Each player has their own pool constantly in front of them. Dice are never “spent” or “used up” They stay in front of you, and stay in the same state they were in. It’s only referenced by cards. You can think of it as your RNG seed for the round.

There are 5 types of dice that can be in your pool: Melee (Black), Venus/Earth (Yellow), Mars/Fire (Red), Jupiter/Wind (Purple), and Mercury/Water (Blue).

First, each character has an Element. This is listed on their character card under “element:”. If it is anything other than “None”, they always have one die of that type in their dice pool.

In addition, your dice pool is modified by the weapon you have equipped Currently, the dice provided by weapons is iconographic. Here are what the symbols mean:

 Venus

 Mars

 Jupiter

 Mercury

 Elemental (Sol/Wildcard)

 Melee

Each of these icons on the card provides 1 of that type of die. The Sol icon means the weapon grants a die of the character’s element that equips it.

Last, add 1 elemental die for each djinni you have equipped of that djinni’s element. If that djinni is ever put into recovery mid-combat, you lose the die it provided until it comes back.

Cards interact with your dice roll in one of 3 ways:

Power

Your “power” is the number of dice you have of a given type. If a card references your Venus Power for example, it is a number equal to the number of Venus dice in your pool, whether they are charged or not. The same goes for things like “Melee Power” (number of black dice in your pool). There is also “Elemental Power” which is the number of elemental dice of any type in your pool.

(e.g. Elemental Power is the number of non-melee dice in your pool, whereas Mars Power is just the number of Mars dice.)

Charged

Charged dice are defined by your weapon. Each weapon type has a unique threshold for charging dice (dice of a certain value, even numbers, pairs, etc). After you roll your dice at the beginning of turn you can treat any dice that meet this condition as “charged”. Charging doesn’t do anything on its own, as with the other criteria here, but it is a common one for cards to reference.

(e.g. All Charge = every die in your pool that meets your weapon’s charge criteria)

Value

Certain cards refer to the actual rolled value of the dice. Some abilities may increase or decrease the “value” of a die, and sometimes this only counts in certain situations. Make sure to read the effect carefully! Usually an effect referencing values will be doing damage/healing equal to the number showing on a dice. Often the highest or lowest rolled one. If it says “Values” it wants the sum of the values of all the dice it mentions

(e.g. Highest Jupiter Value = The Jupiter die that rolled the highest number. If there’s a 6 it’s probably that one. But it has to be the mentioned type, or it doesn’t count)

Die Evaluation

Cards that reference any of the above values will use another keyword system. If you see text in light green, it is a die criteria check. It wants you to add up some amount of the above and tell you how much of it to use.

Assign - If the word Assign is present as part of the evaluation, it means you select a number of dice in your pool equal to the number of targets your ability affects. You may not use a die twice or target an enemy twice unless the ability says so. These effects usually do different amounts of damage or healing based on which die you chose for each target.

Split - If the word Split is present, it means you add up all the damage or healing the ability tells you to do and spread it out however you want amongst the enemies. Abilities with this keyword do not do their damage to each target individually, they only do their damage as a big sum that you split.

Highest/Lowest - If an ability checks the value of a die, it will sometimes tell you to use the highest value or the lowest value (or sometimes both!)

In addition: The card will end by telling you what subset of your pool it’s looking at. If it says Venus, you check the keywords against just the Venus dice. If it says All, you check every die in your pool. And if it says Elemental, any die that isn’t a melee die is checked.

Some examples:

Assign Jupiter Values: Select a Jupiter die for each target and do damage equal to the face value of the die chosen, without doubling up on targets or dice. (If you have a 3 a 4 and 5, and there are three targets, you would do 3 to one, 4 to another, and 5 to the last one, for example)

If an ability targets 3, but you don’t have 3 dice of that type, you can only hit as many enemies as you have dice.

Venus Charge: The number of charged venus dice in your pool

Highest Mercury Value: The face value of the Mercury die that rolled the highest

Split Mars Values: Add up the total value of all Mars dice and split that damage up amongst the targets.

All Charge: The number of charged dice of any type

All Values: The sum of the face values of all dice in your pool

Keywords

All cards in the game with abilities or effects have been reworded to use a new keyword system. Effects are now denoted with a keyword defining when they activate and special conditions or targets.

Each ability is tagged with the activation condition and then two slashes (//) and then either the target or timing of the ability. Here are the possible tags:

Target // : This keyword denotes that the effect targets a character on the opposing team. Enemies target players, and players target enemies! After the // is what is done to that target (usually damage)

Target x // : Same as above, but with a number after it, this targets more than one player. The effect applies to each member individually unless otherwise stated. All targets for player attacks must be adjacent! Dead enemies still count as an enemy and can’t be skipped over for this.

Party // : The effect targets every member of the character’s party. The effect applies to each member individually unless otherwise stated.

Self // : This effect applies only to the one using it. Another player cannot be targeted, and this doesn’t count as “targeting one player”

Target Adept // : The effect targets a player, it cannot target an enemy. Only abilities tagged with Target Adept can target other players. In addition, there will occasionally be an additional qualifier (eg. Target Downed Adept), in order to select a target, it must meet this condition if it is present. (Revive can only be cast on a downed adept, for example)

All Opposing // : Same as above but it targets all members of the opponent’s party. As with before, enemies target the player’s party, and players target the enemy team.

Auto // : This ability is triggered by a specific event. After the // is when this ability CAN activate. You may choose not to activate it then (for example, if it is a spell that activates right before drawing) but once you’ve chosen you may not cast it again until its trigger comes up!

Passive // : This effect stays in effect for the specified amount of time. Passive abilities linger instead of immediately resolving. Think things like 1 round of damage reduction or momentary dice pool changes.

Countdown // : Same as Passive, except that it is locked and cannot be activated again until the specified period of time is done. The effect lingers, but often these effects decay over time. On a spell, that spell cannot be cast on ANYONE until its countdown is complete. If on a Djinni, that djinni does not recover (flip face up) while it has countdown counters on it.

Condition : Some cards in addition to the above keywords will have a condition. Conditions are like permanent passives. They are non-optional, and generally change the way the card interacts with the normal game mechanics.

Some abilities combine multiple different keywords! If you see text that is indented, it applies to the keyword above it that isn’t indented. For example:

Target // 2 Mars Damage:
        Countdown // 3 rounds:
                Target // 1 Mars Damage

This ability would immediately deal 2 Mars damage to a chosen enemy. The card for this ability then gets 3 Countdown tokens. For the next 3 rounds (the next 3 times your turn comes up in the order) it loses 1 countdown token and the same enemy take an additional 1 damage. While it is taking damage from this effect, this ability cannot be activated again, as it has countdown tokens.

(Note above: when there is a target keyword nested under another target, they are the same instance of the effect and target the same opponent! The second keyword is just a way to refer to that enemy again later)

Another example:
Target // 2 Jupiter Damage
Target // 2 Venus Damage

This ability immediately does 2 Jupiter damage to an enemy and then 2 Venus damage to an enemy. They can be the same enemy, or different enemies. They aren’t attached and therefore are separate targets.

Turn order

Most of the game is in Combat. During combat, the player with the first turn token (see Combat) goes first, and once all adepts have gone, then the Enemy Turn happens.

Each player’s turn follows the following order:

  1. PP regen - Add 1 PP to your counter (Some effects may add to this)
  2. Roll - Roll your entire dice pool
  3. Action - Use ONE Action
  4. Djinni Flip - Flip up one face down Djinni (unless their condition states otherwise), Flip face down a djinni that was unleashed this turn, and then recover one djinni from the recovery deck in that order

Stat Decay: Some effects will grant you an Attack or Defense token that modifies your ATK and DEF stat up or down. After the Enemy Turn, remove one DEF token (up or down) from each player if it exists. At the end of each player’s turn, remove an ATK token[a] (up or down).

Please note: Stat decay ONLY affects tokens. Permanent stat changes from armor don’t decay!

For example: Ivan ends his turn with 2 ATK down tokens and 3 DEF up tokens (-2, +3). At the end of the enemy turn, Ivan’s stat decays and he now has 2 ATK down and 2 DEF up tokens (-2,+2), and after his turn ends his ATK stat decays and he is at 1 ATK down token and 2 DEF up tokens (-1,+2)

On your turn, for step 3, you may do one action, your choices for this action are...

Actions:

Attack

Do damage to one opponent. The amount of damage is listed on your weapon card next to “Attack:”. It is usually your ATK stat with an amount added on.

Use an Item

Activate the “Use:” effect of an Item in your hand. Many heal and restore status conditions on other players. However, some do damage and others have special effects.

You can instead equip a weapon or armor card from your hand as your “Use an Item” action. If you don’t have an empty slot to equip an item, this action may be used to replace an item. The item that was equipped goes to your hand

After a regular item is used it is discarded.

Cast Psynergy

Cast a Psynergy spell you have previously learned.  In order to cast, you must subtract the full required cost from your PP total before resolving. Spell effects vary from damage options to more rare status or stat changes.

Many Psynergy cards are multi-tiered with multiple levels. Higher levels are able to be cast when you have multiple copies of the card learned. (e.g. “Quake” allows you to cast “Earthquake” if you know it twice)

To cast a spell, a die of the same element must be present in your dice pool (it doesn’t have to be charged). You can always learn any spell of any element, whether you can cast it or not.

For example. Isaac has 2 Venus djinn, a Mercury djinni, and a Mars djinni. His Mars djinni is in recovery (See Summon). He wants to cast Flare for 3 PP, but can’t, as he currently doesn’t have a Mars die in his pool. He can cast Douse though, as he has a Mercury die.

Unleash a Djinni

Activate the “Unleash:” effect of a Djinni card equipped to you.


Alternatively, you may select a face down Djinni card and flip it face up. This still counts as a Djinni Unleash, and must be done as an action

At the end of your turn, if you selected Unleash a Djinni this turn, that djinni is flipped face down.

Summon

Cast a Summon your party knows. In order to summon, you must select a number of djinn matching the elements in the summons cost. These can be yours alone or spread amongst the party. Some summons require more djinn than one player can have.

Djinn that are used to pay for a summon cost are then placed into recovery. Place them face down. At the end of your turn, after the recovery step outlined below put the die they provide you on top of them, they are now recovering. This die is not part of your dice pool until the djinni under it recovers.

At the end of every turn, after you would (or do) flip a Djinni face up, one Djinni recovers. Take the die on top of it and put it back into your dice pool. If you haven’t recovered them all by the end of combat, you must wait until the first turn of the next combat, or Go to Town.

No Djinn recover at the end of combat.

For example: Felix has 2 Venus djinn and 2 Jupiter djinn. They summon Flora, which costs 1 Venus djinni and 2 Jupiter djinn.

Felix activates the summon’s effect (do damage to opponents equalling the total amount of Jupiter dice among all players. Include the dice given by Djinn you use for this summon in this!) And then flips one of his Venus Djinn and both of his Jupiter Djinn face down.

His dice pool is reduced, Felix loses 1 Venus die and 2 Jupiter die, as they were added to the pool by the Djinn that are recovering. At the end of his turn, after his normal Djinn recovery, he puts the dice for the Djinn that were used to summon on top of them, signifying that their recovery has started.

At the end of his next turn he decides to recover 1 Jupiter Djinni, and his dice pool gets the locked Jupiter die added to it as a result.

Floors

Each lighthouse has a certain number of Floors that must be completed in order to get to the final boss and complete the chapter. Each floor is represented as a card with one or more challenges on it that must be completed in order to advance to the next floor. The floor cards are shuffled into a deck and randomly challenged one by one. Once you are on a floor, you may not choose another one until it is completed. You also may not go to town after entering a floor. (See Going to Town)

Floors can have multiple challenges on them, and multiple types of challenges. Each card will also specify how many need to be completed to move on.

Not all challenges need to be completed! If you are on a floor that only needs 1 out of 2 challenges to be completed and you’ve completed one, you are free to move on to the next floor...

However, if you are the danger seeking sort, you are also welcome to continue to try the remaining challenges on the card. Each challenge can only be attempted once, however.

Combat Challenge

The card will show “Combat:” and then a number of enemies to fight. Lay out that many enemy cards on the board left to right, then initiate combat. (see Combat).

Psynergy Puzzle

If a floor card has a psynergy puzzle at all, flip the top card face up on the psynergy puzzle deck when it is revealed. The puzzle card will show how to solve it, and solving it satisfies the challenge for the floor.

Some puzzles are “traps” which means they impart a passive bonus to the enemies on that floor until it is cleared.

Some floor cards will specify a specific puzzle, if these are shown then search for that specific puzzle card and put it on top of the deck.

Boss challenge

The final floor of every lighthouse, as well as a certain number of random floors in the lighthouse itself, contain a boss challenge. The card will specify a specific boss card to put into play. In addition, it may specify additional enemies that are put into play alongside it.

Combat

The main bulk of the game is in combat. When starting a combat challenge, lay out monsters left to right equal to the number shown on the challenge, plus any applicable effects.

After the monsters are all laid out, combat starts with the player with the first turn token.

Combat goes in order through all the players, and then the enemies. Each player takes their turn as specified in Turn Order above. After all players have taken their turn, the enemy turn begins.

Enemy Turns

At the start of the enemy turn, decay the enemy DEF stat, removing one DEF token from each enemy, if one exists. Then, enemies take their turns in order left to right, and resolve their decisionmaking in the following way:

  1. Select a move

Enemies select what move they will use by rolling the die shown at the top of the card (e.g. “1d4”), roll this and select the ability corresponding to the outcome. 1 is usually a basic attack, for example.

  1. Select a target

Roll a d4 to select which player is targeted. (1 = player 1, 2 = player 2, etc)

  1. Resolve attack

Apply the effect of the attack to the selected player(s)

  • Some actions target 3 players, they hit everyone except the rolled player
  • Some actions target all players, skip the target roll (or ignore it)
  • Some actions target the opponents party instead, such as healing or buffs

NOTE: If a move targets a downed player - the move still goes through, but nothing happens to the downed player. This is also true if a move targets an empty player slot

At the end of the enemy turn, decay the enemy ATK stat, removing one ATK token from each enemy if they exist.

Boss Turns

Bosses function slightly differently to regular enemies. They resolve their turns the same as regular enemies with the following exceptions:

  1. Bosses act on “Boss Turns” in addition to enemy turns
  2. Boss turns are between every player’s turn, every fourth boss turn is a regular enemy turn (after the first round)
  3. The first boss turn is before the first player turn

The turn order for all combats can be visualized as:
Boss > Player 1 > Boss > Player 2 > Boss > Player 3 > Boss > Player 4 > Enemy > Player 1 etc

In normal combat, only the enemy turn and player turns are counted, as only boss monsters act on Boss turns. But note that in boss combats Bosses also act on enemy turns!

Damage

Damage is calculated in a specific order, the order is as follows:

Outgoing damage:
1) base damage

2) buffs/equipment (att up)

3) debuffs/equipment (att down)

4) unleash/djinn/bonuses

Incoming damage:

1) weakness

2) buffs/equipment/resistance (def up)

3) debuffs/equipment (def down)

4) unleash/djinn/bonuses (granite/flash)


First, calculate the damage modification going out (that is to say, any damage curses or buffs on an opponent that change the amount of damage they’re inflicting.)

Then, calculate any changes to the amount of damage that you take. Such as Djinni, armor, and tokens. (for example, since flash is calculated after all other damage reduction, any attack that would do ANY amount of damage to you before flash will do 1 after flash, even if you have something that could reduce that 1 to a 0)

Resistance

Adepts (and enemies) are resistant to their own element. As a player, if you receive elemental damage you are resistant to, lower the damage done by your Modifier. Your elemental modifier for an element is your power for that element divided by 2 and rounded down. For example, with 4 Mars dice, a player with a Mars character would take 2 less damage when hit with Mars damage. But with 4 Mars dice a Mercury character will have a Mars modifier of 2, but it will not apply to resistance as they don’t have a resistance to Mars.

Enemies by default always take 1 less damage to their own element. Although some curses can increase this. This still only applies to their element.

Weakness

When an enemy is hit with a damage type it is weak to, increase the amount of damage you deal when hitting them with that element by that element’s modifier: your power for that element divided by two rounded down.

Both of these values have a minimum of 1. If you are weak or resistant you will always modify damage by at least 1!

Enemies can be considered to always have an elemental modifier of 1 if it ever comes up.

Death

When a player’s health is reduced to 0 they are downed. When a player is downed all tokens are removed from them, and their turns are skipped. While a player is downed you can consider them not in the turn order at all, so there is 1 less boss turn while they are down.

A player can be revived by receiving healing at any time. If a player is revived before their turn they are welcome to act as if they had not been downed that round. In addition, there are some items and abilities that can only target downed adepts.

If a downed adept is targeted by an enemy attack, no damage is done, but the attack still targets them.

Status Conditions

Both enemies and adepts can be afflicted with certain status conditions that may modify their turn

Sleep

A character inflicted with sleep cannot do anything on their turn. Enemies and players roll a d6 at the start of their turn and wake up if they roll a 4 or higher. When a character wakes up, they may take their turn as normal.

Poison

A character inflicted with poison takes 1 damage at the end of every turn (each player and the enemy turn). They do not take damage on boss turns except for the one enemy turn each round. Poison does not go away on its own and must be healed with items or by going to town

Venom

Venom functions identically to poison except it does 3 damage at the end of every turn instead of 1. Anything that heals poison also heals Venom

Stun

A stunned character must roll a 4 or higher on a d6 before taking an attack, psynergy, or djinni action. Items and summons can be used as normal. If an enemy rolls under a 4 their turn is skipped.

When stun is applied, the stunned character gains 3 stun tokens. At the end of each of their turns one stun token is removed. As long as stun tokens exist, the stun debuff applies. If stun is reapplied, the number of stun tokens is set back to 3.

Delusion

An enemy who is deluded rolls a d6 instead of a d4 when deciding a target.

On a 1-4: Target the players as normal

On a 5-6: Target the enemy on the left (5) or the enemy on the right (6)

Seal

If a character’s psynergy is sealed, they are unable to use a psynergy action on their turn.

For players: You cannot use the “Psynergy” or “Summon” actions on your turn

For enemies: If a “psynergy” action is rolled (denoted with the psynergy seal icon next to the name), the attack is rerolled. If only one attack is eligible to be used, you can skip rolling and use that.

Haunted

If a character is haunted, put 2 haunt tokens on them. At the end of each enemy turn, remove 1. As soon as the last token is removed, that character is downed (health is reduced to 0)

Bosses are immune to this status condition

Attempt Rolls

When inflicting status, some abilities will mention “attempting” to inflict it. When inflicting a status this way roll a d6: on a 4+ that status is inflicted.

Watch out: All status inflicted onto bosses are downgraded to attempts. No status can be automatically inflicted on a boss.

In addition, bosses recover from all status conditions automatically after a set number of boss/enemy turns. Each boss has a different maximum amount of turns listed on their card next to the “delusion” icon

Going to Town

After completing a floor, instead of going to the next floor right away you may instead choose to go to town. You may only go to town after completing a floor and before going to the next floor. Once a floor has been started, it must be completed before going to town.

When going to town, select a town card to visit. Each town has some variance in items offered and rewards for visiting them. In each town, take the following actions in this order:

Recover

When arriving in ANY town, restore all status conditions, refill all players’ HP and PP to full, and recover all djinn in the recovery deck.

Find

Each town card has a list of things you “find” in the town. This can be a level up, a djinni joining the party, drawing item cards, etc. At this point, get them. Do all card drafting here.

Buy

At each town are different shops, offering the ability to purchase items that can help you along your way. This is the only way to recover items from the discard after they have been purchased:

Weapons/Armor: Buy equipment found in this chapter from the discard pile.

Items: Buy consumable items that have been previously used or discarded this chapter from the discard pile, usually less expensive than gear.

Artifact: Buy a weapon or armor from a previous chapter and Improve* it. You cannot buy consumables from a previous chapter.

Psynergy: Learn a psynergy from the available cards on sale. When this option is available, reveal 5 Psynergy cards when the town is entered.

Summon: Buy a summon card from the available cards on sale. When this option is available, reveal 2 Summon cards when the town is entered.

If a town has Psynergy/Summons for sale, you can only purchase the revealed card(s)

*Improvement: Whenever a weapon is purchased from a previous chapter, it gets 1 improvement token for each chapter back it was (i.e. in chapter 2, if you buy a chapter 1 weapon, it gets one improvement token). When improving a weapon, choose one of the following improvements to apply:

- Unleash - Your unleash now applies an additional flat 2 damage whenever it is triggered. If the weapon did not previously have an unleash, it gains the following ability:

“Unleash // Solo 6 - Improvement: Attack does +2 damage of your character’s element”

- Melee - Your weapon grants 1 additional melee die

- Elemental - Your weapon grants 1 additional elemental die of a type it already provided. If the weapon did not provide elemental dice this improvement cannot be taken.

NOTE: You can select a different improvement for each token! Buying a chapter 1 weapon in chapter 4 gives you 3 tokens to apply however you want!

Learning Magic (Psynergy/Summons/Djinn)

Learning Psynergy

When psynergy is learned (either from an event or a level up) lay out 10 psynergy cards from the top of their deck face up. All players that are learning psynergy may select one of the revealed cards to add to their known spells.

If there are no spells you wish to learn, once per chapter you may opt instead to search the deck for another copy of a psynergy you already know, upgrading it to the next tier (if one exists)

In addition to the above, before learning a spell you may also trade any psynergy card you have learned for the first level of a spell you don’t already have any levels in. You may do this as many times as you want. You still must gain another psynergy after doing this, as this does not replace your learning of a psynergy.

After all players have learned their spell, replace any spells taken out of the deck with another copy from the extra psynergy deck

Meeting Djinn

When a djinni joins the party, reveal the top 3 cards from the djinn deck.

All players must work together to select one djinni to join the party permanently, which is then immediately equipped to an adept.

No adept can have 2 more djinn than any other player (with the exception of characters that cannot gain djinn), but they can be traded when not in combat (even on a floor)

If all adepts have 4 djinn equipped, then one must be replaced in order to equip it. When this happens, take the old one and shuffle it back into the djinn deck.

You may also pass and select no Djinn.

Learning Summons

When learning a summon, reveal two summon cards from the summon deck and select one as a party. Put it in an area accessible to all players face up. Any player may now summon this card using any player’s djinn as payment. Shuffle the summon that wasn’t picked back into the summon deck

The Lighthouse and Tracker

Each Lighthouse is organized into 8 floors that are defined by the Floor deck, but your progress through the Lighthouse can be tracked with the Lighthouse Tracker.

The tracker is a small board with lines of spaces representing the individual floors. Every time you clear a floor, move your party token to the next space. If a space has an icon on it, there is either a reward or a curse associated with that floor.

Each floor card has a key icon in the top left corner, denoting if it is a regular floor or a special floor. Shuffle the cards with a blue key icon together to form the floor deck, and keep the floors with a red key separate. These are used for specific areas (See Separated Sections below)

Rewards

Floors with a blue background and an icon provide something when the floor is CLEARED. Most times this will be a level up (a green gem icon) in which all players gain 1 level and resolve their level up mechanic. Some floors will have a djinni icon (1 djinni joins the party) or a summon tablet (learn 1 summon). See the icon glossary for more information about each icon and its associated reward.

Curses

Floors with a red background are curse floors. Whenever one of these floors is ENTERED, roll one of the curse dice. The icon that shows on the die applies a curse to all subsequent combats.

The possible curses are:

  • All enemies on all floors gain +1 ATK
  • All enemies on all floors take an additional 1 less damage when attacked with their own element (+1 Resistance)
  • All enemies on all floors gain +3 Max HP (Bosses gain +10 HP instead)

Once a curse is rolled, there is no way to change it, it applies for the rest of the chapter.

At the end of the chapter choose one curse to remain in effect at the start of the next chapter. This becomes a Permanent Curse and applies to all subsequent chapters

Separated Sections

Some sections on the tracker show a number of spaces that are separated from the rest of the tracker. These will generally be labelled and refer to specific floors that are not shuffled into the rest of the floor deck (denoted with a red key icon in the corner). When you arrive at these floors, go to the floors designated for these sections instead of drawing from the floor deck. These usually have set encounters/puzzles/bosses instead of the more randomly drawn ones.

These sections will be labelled with the floor that is associated with it. If a floor is labelled “Start” you should draw from the floor deck until you reach a separated section.

For example, Venus Lighthouse always begins with “Venus Entry 1” “Venus Entry 2” and “Venus Entry 3” before going through the remaining floors randomized. This separated section is labelled “Venus Entry” and the next floor after Venus Entry 3 is labelled with “Start”

Aerie

At the end of the tracker is a 1 space long separated section. This is the Aerie, and each lighthouse has one which contains the final boss.

Advance to the aerie once the floor deck is empty and any other requirements for the chapter are complete.

Final Boss

The final boss acts like any other boss and gets turns and immunities the same as normal bosses. After defeating the final boss do not draw from the treasure deck, you’ve won the chapter!

As a reward for completing the chapter, reveal 4 cards from the boss loot deck and as a party give adept on of the rewards. These will permanently alter stats or be powerful items and remain persistent between chapters

Starting a new chapter

When a chapter is done, note down your learned psynergy, equipped djinn and equipment, and any boss loot cards that your character has. All consumable items (and leftover gold) are not saved.

When starting the next chapter, each character begins with their Max HP, 3 PP for each chapter (in chapter 3 they would start with 9 PP), and their starting consumable (the second item listed in their starting equipment). Do not overwrite what weapon they had equipped in the previous chapter. Then equip any items they had equipped from the chapter decks they originated from.

GLOSSARY

Key Terms

Adept - A player or that player’s character.

Aerie - The top of a lighthouse/The final floor of the lighthouse

Affinity - The amount of dice in the pool of a given type (e.g. Venus Affinity is the number of Venus dice). This applies even out of turn.

ATK - Attack stat, all basic attacks do this as their base value before being added to by a weapon

ATK/DEF tokens - Token that represents a temporary +1 or -1 to the ATK or DEF stat.

Boss Loot - Permanent treasure earned from a boss fight

Charged Dice - Dice in the dice pool that meet the weapon card’s condition for being charged

Curse Die - Die used to select a curse on a curse floor

Curses - Persistent modifiers applied to all future floors.

DEF - Defense stat, all incoming damage has this value subtracted from it.

Delusion - Status condition - Enemies might attack each other

Dice Pool - The collection of dice a player has at their disposal. It is rolled at the beginning of turn

Haunt - Status condition - marked for death, will die in 3 turns

Lighthouse Tracker - Used to track progress through the dungeon, lists which floors have rewards and curses

Poison - Status condition - Does 1 damage at the end of every player turn and the enemy turn (not boss turns)

Psynergy - A spell/A spell card. Each copy of a spell grants one of its levels. Players have access to any level they have learned

Psynergy Puzzle - An optional challenge on a floor that can be cleared by casting a given psynergy

Recovery Deck - A deck of djinn that have been used in a summon. Each player has one, and only their own djinn go to it.

Round - A complete cycle where each players act once and all enemies act once. Ends after the enemy turn. Effects that last 1 round end at the end of the player’s turn that it started on

Seal (Psynergy Seal) - Status condition - Cannot cast Psynergy

Separate Section - A separated section on the Lighthouse Tracker - Uses the red key cards

Sleep - Status condition - Must roll 4+ on targeting die or turn is skipped. Cleared when a 4+ is rolled.

Stat Decay - ATK and DEF stats change one step toward zero at the end of every turn

Stun - Status condition - Must roll 4+ on d6 or certain actions are skipped

Subtype - What type of card it is among its card type (e.g. Short Sword is a subtype of Weapons, which are themselves a subtype of Item cards)

Summon - A powerful spell using djinn from your party as payment

Targeting Die - The die used to select what move an enemy uses, Varies by enemy

Turn - A single player’s action, each player gets one and the enemies get one. Not to be confused with a round

Unleash - A card’s special effect. On djinn cards this is their active ability. On weapon cards this is their passive abilities. To use the “Unleash” action is to activate and/or flip over a djinni card.

Value(s) - The actual number showing on the face of a die
Venom - Status condition - Does 3 damage at the end of every player turn and the enemy turn (not boss turns)
